## 1. The Fundraising Decision Matrix

### DON'T Raise Money If:

| Situation | Reasoning | Real Examples |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| **First SaaS Company** | Learn with your own money; mistakes won't cost you control | Most failed startups |
| **Want Maximum Control** | Every investor means less autonomy in decision-making | Craigslist (Craig Newmark kept 100%) |

| **Building Lifestyle Business** | $5-50M ARR businesses that print cash | OnlyFans (88% profit margins) |
| **No Competition Pressure** | Time to build slowly and sustainably | Valve (Steam, privately held) |
| **Already Have Revenue** | Use customer money to grow | Surge (Alex's company, bootstrap) |

**Outcome**: Keep 80-100% equity, full control, sustainable growth

| Situation | Reasoning | Real Examples |

## 11. Alex Becker's Golden Rules

> "These are the non-negotiables I wish I knew before making funding decisions."

### Rule #1: Never Raise on Your First SaaS
**Why**: You don't know what you're doing yet. Learn on your own dime.

**Exception**: You've built and exited companies before (even if not SaaS).

---

### Rule #2: If You Don't NEED to Raise, Don't
**Why**: Giving up equity is permanent. You can always raise later.

**Test**: Can you get to $10K MRR on your own? If yes, you probably don't need funding.

---

### Rule #3: Never Take Money from Bad Investors
**Why**: Better to fail alone than with a bad partner.

**Bad Investor Signs**:
- Pressures you to close quickly
- Doesn't understand your market
- References speak poorly of them
- Wants control clauses

---

### Rule #4: Raise When You Have Leverage, Not Desperation
**Why**: Best terms come when you don't "need" the money.

**Timing**: Raise when:
- Revenue is growing 20%+ month-over-month
- Multiple investors competing for your deal
- You could survive without raising

---

### Rule #5: Know Your "Why" Before You Raise
**Why**: Investors will ask. You need clarity.

**Good Reasons**:
- "Competitors raised $50M, we need to move faster"
- "We have 300% YoY growth, need to hire to keep up"
- "Enterprise customers require features that need 10 engineers"

**Bad Reasons**:
- "Everyone else is raising"
- "We're running out of money"
- "I don't know how to make this profitable"
